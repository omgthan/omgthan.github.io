  • Education

    I recently (May 2025) graduated from the University of Kansas with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. I finished a 3.710 cumulative GPA and appearing on KU's engineering honor roll (minimum requirement of 3.75 semester GPA across 12 graded credit hours) in four of my eight semesters in the school.

    While I didn't have a particular focus of study within the field, some of the CS electives I took that I found particularly interesting were Compiler Construction (EECS 665), Software Reverse Engineering (EECS 695), Digital Systems Design (EECS 443), and Programming Languages (EECS 662). Some non-CS classes I enrolled in that I took interest in included Greek and Roman Mythology (CLSX 148), Introductory Linguistics (ANTH 106), Contemporary Astronomy (ASTR 191), and Language and Mind (LING 110).

  • Interests

    My studies weren't narrowed to one particular area within computer science, but I did find myself more intrigued working with lower-level code. I found Digital Logic design and Embedded Systems (EECS 388) to be interesting because I was writing code (in VHDL and C, respectively) and seeing it work on a tangible device. I found Compiler Construction and Software Reverse Engineering interesting because I could see the inner workings of a compiler, understanding both how a program was compiled into an even lower-level language (x64) and how the assembly code itself functions.

    The interest I took in some of the non-CS electives I was in stems from an enjoyment that I already have in those subjects. I'm not a huge nerd in any of these topics, but I do enjoy learning about Greek and Roman mythology, space, and linguistics. I also love basketball, and I try to watch every Kansas Jayhawks basketball game.

    My biggest hobby, though, is playing video games, and they are the main reason I fell in love with coding. Growing up, I was enamored with the idea of one day being able to make my own, and I became inspired to learn how to program so I can bring my ideas to fruition. As I got older, and especially as I started at college, this expanded from making video games to programming anything. Coding became my outlet to create, with the keyboard as my paintbrush and the computer as my canvas.

  • Experience

    While I am still in the search for my first programming position, I do at least have prior work experience with part time jobs, from which I have learned valuable skills in both leadership and teamwork, directing coworkers and collaborating with team members to operate as efficiently as possible. I have also learned the importance of time management and prioritizing and addressing customer concerns.

    My programming experience is primarily in C/C++ and Python. I have also spent some time programming with HTML, JavaScript, and SQL. My experience with these comes mostly from classes I took while at KU, as well as brief interactions with Haskell and Racket. Outside of school work, I have put major focus into learning GDScript, the scripting language used in the Godot game engine.
